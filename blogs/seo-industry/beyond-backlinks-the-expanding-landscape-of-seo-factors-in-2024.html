While backlinks remain a crucial element of SEO, Google's ranking algorithm is becoming increasingly sophisticated. Here are some **additional factors** to consider in your SEO strategy:

Technical SEO

Ensure your website is technically sound. This includes having a mobile-friendly design, a clean code structure, and fast loading speeds.

Core Web Vitals

Pay attention to Google's **Core Web Vitals**, which measure website performance metrics like Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) and First Input Delay (FID). A user-friendly website experience is key for ranking.

User Engagement

Focus on creating content that keeps users engaged. Track metrics like time on page, bounce rate, and click-through rate to see how users interact with your content.

E-A-T (Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness)

As mentioned in our E-A-T blog, **EAT remains a prominent factor**. By creating high-quality content written by experts in the field, you establish trust and credibility with both Google and your audience.

Schema Markup

Utilize **schema markup** to provide search engines with richer information about your content. This can help your website appear in rich snippets and knowledge panels, increasing visibility.

Mobile-First Indexing

Google now prioritizes mobile versions of websites for indexing. Ensure your mobile site is optimized for search and offers a positive user experience.

Video Optimization

Video content is booming. Optimize your videos with relevant titles, descriptions, and tags to improve search discovery.

Social Media Engagement

While not a direct ranking factor, social media engagement can build brand awareness and trust signals that Google may consider.

Conclusion

The SEO landscape is constantly evolving. By staying informed about the latest trends and incorporating these factors into your strategy, you can ensure your website remains competitive and achieves top rankings in search results.
